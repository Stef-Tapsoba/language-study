## v2.1.0 — Release Notes

**Released:** 2026-03-11

---

### New Features

#### Culture Page
The Culture section now has its own dedicated page with an amber-themed design.
Each language shows a hero banner with its flag, name, and "Cultural Insights"
subtitle. Passages render in article cards with comprehension questions,
vocabulary gloss, and a sticky "Mark as read" bar.

#### Grammar Lesson Detail Page
Grammar lessons are now full pages instead of inline accordions. Clicking any
lesson navigates to `/grammar/:lessonId`, showing the full explanation
(level-appropriate, immersion-aware), all examples with pronunciation buttons,
and a "Mark as complete" button. The Grammar Drill also links directly to the
relevant lesson after revealing an answer, so you can review the rule without
leaving the drill.

#### Reading Hub & Category Pages
The Reading section is now a category hub. Instead of a flat list of all
passages, it shows category cards with completion counters. Each category has
its own themed page:

| Category    | Theme  |
|-------------|--------|
| Everyday    | Blue   |
| Dialogue    | Teal   |
| History     | Stone  |
| Literature  | Purple |
| Culture     | Amber  |

Only categories with content at your current level are shown.

#### Wrong Answer Review
After finishing a drill or level test, a collapsible **"Review mistakes"**
section appears if you got anything wrong:

- **Verb Drill & Grammar Drill** — prompt, your answer (red), correct answer (green)
- **Grammar Drill** — also links to the relevant grammar lesson
- **Level Test** — each missed question with its correct answer
- **Flashcards** — "Review missed words" list with word and translation

#### Level Test Start Screen
The Level Test now opens with a start screen showing your current level badge
and the pass requirement (e.g. _"Pass 12/15 questions to advance to B2"_)
before any questions appear. Retrying after a failed attempt returns you to
the start screen.

---

### Bug Fixes

- **Grammar navigation loop** — Grammar → Lesson → back no longer creates an
  infinite loop. The back button now correctly pops the history stack.
- **Flag rendering on Windows** — Flag images on the Landing Page and Culture
  Page now use CDN images instead of emoji characters, which don't render on
  Windows.
