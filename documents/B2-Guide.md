# B2 Language Coverage Guide
*French, Spanish, Italian, Japanese, Korean*

---

## What B2 Means (Universal Definition)

At **B2 (Upper Intermediate)** level, a learner can:

- Engage in extended conversation with native speakers without strain on either side
- Understand the main ideas of complex texts, including technical writing in their field
- Express ideas fluently and spontaneously with only occasional searching for words
- Produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects
- Explain a viewpoint, giving advantages and disadvantages of various options
- Follow fast speech and understand regional accents with reasonable effort

B2 represents **fluent discussion** — the learner is no longer managing the language but using it to think and communicate naturally.

---

## Romance Languages: French, Spanish, Italian

### Grammar Coverage
- Full subjunctive mastery:
  - Present subjunctive in purpose, doubt, emotion, concession clauses
  - Past subjunctive (imparfait du subjonctif / pasado subjuntivo) for formal/literary register
- Conditional structures at full depth:
  - Present conditional (hypotheticals about the present)
  - Past conditional (hypotheticals about the past: "would have")
  - Mixed conditionals (past condition, present consequence)
- Passive voice in active use
- Sequence of tenses in complex reported speech
- Idiomatic expressions, fixed phrases, and colloquial registers
- Discourse connectors: concession, contrast, emphasis, reformulation

### Core Vocabulary Themes
- Economics, politics, and governance
- Science, technology, and innovation
- Philosophy, ethics, and abstract debate
- Arts, literature, and media criticism
- Formal academic and professional registers

### Functional Skills
- Argue a nuanced position with counter-arguments
- Navigate formal and informal registers deliberately
- Write analytical essays and structured reports
- Understand fast native speech in natural contexts (news, podcasts, film without subtitles)
- Express irony, humour, and cultural nuance

### Language-Specific Notes
- **French**: the formal subjunctive (imparfait du subjonctif) is B2+ but recognition is expected; distinguishing C1-level literary register from spoken B2 register is key; stylistic variation (written vs spoken) becomes a learning focus
- **Spanish**: ser vs estar distinctions reach full mastery including abstract uses; the full range of subjunctive triggers covered; regional vocabulary and accent comprehension expected
- **Italian**: congiuntivo presente and passato in full use; condizionale passato ("avrei voluto"); stylistic register awareness — formal written Italian vs colloquial spoken Italian diverge significantly

## Japanese (B2 / JLPT N2)

### Writing System Progress
- ~1,000 kanji in reading — covers most joyo kanji for everyday life
- Newspapers, standard novels, and formal documents become mostly accessible
- Kanji compounds for abstract vocabulary: 経済 (economy), 政治 (politics), 技術 (technology)

### Grammar Coverage
- Formal written structures: ~において (in the context of), ~に関して (regarding), ~によって (by/due to)
- Causative-passive: ~させられる (to be made to do — strong nuance)
- Expressing concession: ~にもかかわらず (despite), ~ものの (although), ~くせに (even though — critical tone)
- Expressing tendency or habit: ~がちだ (tend to), ~っぽい (rather, somewhat)
- Formal doubt/uncertainty: ~かねない (might well), ~かねる (cannot bring oneself to)
- Nominalisation and clause embedding for academic writing
- Politeness levels in full — keigo recognition (sonkeigo, kenjōgo)
- Register distinction: formal written Japanese vs casual spoken

### Sentence Structure
- Long, embedded sentences with multiple subordinate clauses
- Stylistic compression in written form (omitting subjects, particles in casual speech)
- Full command of wa-ga distinction and topicalisation

### Core Vocabulary Themes
- Economics and business (経済、企業、市場)
- Science and environment (科学、環境問題、エネルギー)
- Society and social issues (少子化、格差、福祉)
- Media and culture (報道、伝統、流行)
- Academic and formal registers

### Functional Skills
- Read news articles and commentary with full comprehension
- Write formal emails, reports, and structured arguments
- Understand fast native speech (news broadcasts, TV dramas)
- Discuss abstract social topics with nuance
- Navigate formal settings (business introductions, keigo lite)

### Common Challenges
- Keigo (formal speech) — active production is rare even at B2 but recognition is expected
- Long embedded clauses require strong working memory for SOV sentence processing
- Register mismatch: formal written Japanese patterns feel unnatural in speech

## Korean (B2 / TOPIK II-4)

### Writing System Progress
- Hangul fully fluent; vocabulary breadth is the primary challenge at B2
- Sino-Korean vocabulary dominates academic and formal registers
- Hanja (Chinese character) recognition is a bonus — not required but helps with vocabulary inference

### Grammar Coverage
- Full honorific system in active production: 존댓말 (formal polite) and 반말 (casual) used deliberately
- Complex reported speech with tense embedding: -다고 했다, -라고 한다
- Concessive structures: -(으)ㄹ지라도 (even if), -더라도 (even if it is the case that)
- Formal causals: -(으)므로 (therefore — written), -기 때문에 in longer argumentation
- Expressing inevitability or strong tendency: -(으)ㄹ 수밖에 없다 (have no choice but to)
- Nominalisation in academic writing: -는 것, -기, -(으)ㅁ
- Discourse markers for argumentation: 그러나, 따라서, 즉, 반면에
- Indirect quotation in all tenses and moods

### Core Vocabulary Themes
- Society and social issues (사회문제, 불평등, 복지)
- Economics and business (경제, 취업, 산업)
- Politics and governance (정치, 제도, 법)
- Science and environment (과학기술, 환경오염, 에너지)
- Culture and identity (문화, 전통, 세대 차이)

### Functional Skills
- Argue a position in writing with structured evidence
- Navigate honorific speech levels deliberately in social situations
- Understand fast conversational Korean (native drama, podcast, interview)
- Read opinion columns and news articles
- Write formal essays and structured academic paragraphs

### Common Challenges
- Active production of honorifics consistently across all verbs and nouns
- Sino-Korean vocabulary explosion — thousands of items with no phonetic pattern aid for English speakers
- Discourse connectors in writing feel unnatural to learners used to conversational Korean only

## Register and Speech Styles

At B2, register control is **fully fluent** — learners do not just follow rules, they make **stylistic choices**. They can read a room, adjust mid-conversation, and produce slang, formal prose, and everything in between. The key new dimension is understanding that **written and spoken registers diverge significantly** in all three language groups.

### Romance Languages
B2 learners move through the full register spectrum fluidly:

| Register | When to use |
|---|---|
| Formal written | Academic essays, official letters, journalism, formal emails |
| Formal spoken | Business meetings, presentations, public speech, speaking to authority |
| Neutral polite | Default everyday — colleagues, strangers, service contexts |
| Informal spoken | Friends, casual social settings, relaxed workplaces |
| Colloquial / slang | Close friends, casual text messages, social media — understood and used naturally |

**Key B2 awareness**: Written French/Spanish/Italian is structurally more complex than spoken — longer sentences, more subjunctive, passive constructions, formal connectors. Learners recognise this divergence and adjust their register based on medium (writing vs speech) not just audience.

Regional variation becomes relevant at B2: comprehending different accents (Latin American Spanish, Swiss French, regional Italian) and understanding that vocabulary choices differ by region (e.g., *voiture* vs *char*, *ordenador* vs *computadora*).

### Japanese
B2 learners have **full register awareness** and beginning **keigo production** in controlled settings.

| Register | When to use |
|---|---|
| Keigo — sonkeigo (尊敬語) | Describing the actions of customers, superiors, guests — elevates the other person |
| Keigo — kenjōgo (謙譲語) | Describing your own actions to a superior — lowers yourself |
| Teineigo (丁寧語 / です/ます) | General polite default — most adult interactions |
| Plain form | Close friends, family, casual social settings, internal monologue |
| Formal written | Documents, news articles, formal letters — distinct grammar patterns (~において, ~によって) |

**Key B2 awareness**: Formal written Japanese feels unnatural in speech. Learners recognise register mismatch and avoid using written-register structures in casual or even standard polite conversation.

### Korean
B2 learners have **full honorific production** across all social contexts — this is the defining B2 milestone for Korean.

| Register | When to use |
|---|---|
| 합쇼체 (-습니다) | Formal presentations, job interviews, news broadcasting, official public speech |
| 해요체 (-아요/-어요) | Everyday polite — most adult interactions, workplace default, strangers |
| 해체 / 반말 | Close friends, younger people, children, casual texting |
| Sino-Korean formal vocabulary | Academic writing, business documents, formal news — contrasts with native Korean in casual speech |

**Key B2 challenge**: Consistently applying honorifics to *all* verbs and nouns in a sentence (not just the sentence-final verb) under conversational pressure. Learners know the rules but may drop honorific noun forms or forget mid-sentence when speaking fast.

## Comparative Overview

| Feature | Romance Languages | Japanese | Korean |
|------|------------------|----------|--------|
| Defining B2 Grammar | Full subjunctive + past conditional | Formal written structures + causative-passive | Full honorifics + complex nominalisation |
| Script Challenge | None | ~1,000 kanji | Vocabulary depth (Sino-Korean) |
| Register Awareness | Formal vs informal stylistic distinction | Formal written vs casual spoken divergence | Honorific speech level production |
| Core Vocabulary | Abstract: politics, science, ethics | 経済, 社会, 技術 compounds | Sino-Korean academic vocabulary |
| Defining Skill | Fluent argumentation and nuanced opinion | Reading newspapers; formal writing | Sustained formal written argumentation |

---

## Summary

- B2 learners hold their own in any conversation with native speakers, though subtleties are still missed
- The subjunctive (Romance), formal written grammar (Japanese), and honorific production (Korean) are the defining B2 milestones
- Vocabulary depth — particularly abstract and formal registers — is often the main bottleneck at B2, not grammar
- Authentic input (news, podcasts, film without subtitles) becomes consistently accessible
- B2 is the entry point for work and academic contexts in the target language
