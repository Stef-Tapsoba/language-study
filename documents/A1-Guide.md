# A1 Language Coverage Guide  
*French, Spanish, Italian, Japanese, Korean*

---

## What A1 Means (Universal Definition)

At **A1 (Beginner)** level, a learner can:

- Understand and use familiar everyday expressions
- Introduce themselves and others
- Ask and answer simple personal questions
- Interact in a basic way if the other person speaks slowly and clearly
- Read short, simple texts
- Write basic phrases and sentences

A1 is focused on **survival communication**, not fluency.

---

## Romance Languages: French, Spanish, Italian

These languages share a similar grammatical foundation and learning structure.

### Writing & Pronunciation
- Latin alphabet
- Accent marks (é, ñ, à, è, etc.)
- Pronunciation rules introduced early  
  - Spanish & Italian are more phonetic than French

### Grammar Coverage
- Gendered nouns (masculine / feminine)
- Definite & indefinite articles
- Singular and plural forms
- Present tense only
- Verb conjugation for:
  - *to be*
  - *to have*
  - Regular verbs (-er / -ar / -are)
- Basic negation
- Question formation

### Sentence Structure
- Subject + Verb + Object
- Simple statements and questions

### Core Vocabulary Themes
- Greetings and politeness
- Numbers (0–100)
- Days, months, seasons
- Family and relationships
- Food and drinks
- Directions and locations
- Nationalities and professions

### Functional Skills
- Introduce yourself
- Talk about your job or studies
- Order food and drinks
- Ask for directions
- Express likes and dislikes

### Language-Specific Notes
- **French**: Silent letters, pronunciation challenges
- **Spanish**: Verb conjugation accuracy
- **Italian**: Gender and article agreement

## Japanese (A1 / JLPT N5 Equivalent)

### Writing Systems
- Hiragana (required)
- Katakana (loanwords)
- ~50 basic Kanji

### Grammar Coverage
- No verb conjugation by person
- Present and past tense
- Polite vs plain speech (introductory)
- Core particles:
  - は (topic)
  - を (object)
  - に / で (time and place)

### Sentence Structure
- Subject + Object + Verb
- Topic-based sentences
- Subject omission when context is clear

### Core Vocabulary Themes
- Greetings (formal and casual)
- Numbers and counters
- Food
- Time and dates
- Places
- Daily activities

### Functional Skills
- Self-introduction
- Ordering food
- Asking where things are
- Talking about daily routines
- Expressing basic preferences

### Common Challenges
- Particle usage
- Politeness awareness
- Multiple writing systems

## Korean (A1 / TOPIK I – Level 1)

### Writing System
- Hangul (phonetic and systematic)

### Grammar Coverage
- No verb conjugation by person
- Present and past tense
- Politeness-based verb endings
- Particles:
  - 은/는 (topic)
  - 이/가 (subject)
  - 을/를 (object)
- Introductory honorific awareness

### Sentence Structure
- Subject + Object + Verb
- Frequent subject omission

### Core Vocabulary Themes
- Greetings (formality-based)
- Numbers (native and Sino-Korean)
- Food
- Family
- Places
- Daily activities

### Functional Skills
- Polite self-introduction
- Ordering food
- Asking basic questions
- Talking about routines
- Expressing likes and dislikes

### Common Challenges
- Politeness levels
- Particle distinctions
- Two number systems

## Comparative Overview

| Feature | Romance Languages | Japanese | Korean |
|------|------------------|----------|--------|
| Writing System | Latin alphabet | Hiragana/Katakana/Kanji | Hangul |
| Word Order | SVO | SOV | SOV |
| Verb Conjugation by Person | Yes | No | No |
| Politeness System | Light | Strong | Strong |
| Writing Difficulty | Low–Medium | High | Medium |

---

## Summary

- Romance languages offer faster initial comprehension for English speakers
- Japanese and Korean require structural mindset shifts
- A1 focuses on comprehension, survival communication, and confidence-building
- Mastery of A1 sets the foundation for conversational growth at A2
